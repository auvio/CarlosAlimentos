    • INGREDIENTS

  • 400 gr Force Flour

  • 150 gr Wheat Flour

  • 200 ml of extra virgin olive oil of eco-colmena

  • Half orange peel

  • 1 Teaspoon of anise coffee beans (matalaúva)

  • 1 Teaspoon of sesame coffee (sesame)

  • 100 ml of White Wine

  • 50 ml of Orange Juice

  • Pinch of salt

    • PREPARATION

    Put the oil in a pan with the teaspoons of anise, sesame seeds and orange peel. We let the whole set cool a little (eye that the seeds are not burned or bitter later) we separate and let cool completely. Once cold remove only the orange peel. We reserve.

    In a bowl we put the two flours + juice + wine + pinch of salt + fried oil with the little seeds. Mix first with a wooden spoon and then knead with your hands. We must have a perfect and smooth mass ... it does not stick to the hands or the work table and with these quantities it comes out perfect.

    Once we have the dough we take a portion and with the roller we leave it as finite as possible (without it breaking) .... Do not leave it too thick or the result will be bad. With a glass we make circles ... .. once we have the circle we put one side towards the center and the other equal ... .. we tighten a little so that it does not separate and we already have the pestiño. We do all this until we finish with the dough.

    Fry in plenty of oil (special for frying), the temperature should not be too low or the pestiño will not float ... rather strong but without passing that burns and is very golden and raw inside. Once fried let it rest on a plate with kitchen paper to remove excess oil.

    Once fried we have to enmielarlos ... .. for this we put a little less than half a pot of honey and a tad of water to lower it .... heat over high heat until it is completely diluted ... once liquid, we go down to very low heat and we put the pestiños in several batches ... .. we let them float for a while and with the wooden spoon we submerge them several times .... then we take out and leave in a container (tupper). Once all are covered with honey ... .. with the remaining honey (that of the saucepan) we bathe lightly if you want so that the ones below are not dried ... .. and we have them.
